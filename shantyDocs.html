LEARN SHANTY

OVERVIEW

SHANTY is a syntax to make writing e-books easier. With minimal markup, anything from a short story to a novel can be easily converted into my Nantucket E-Reader format.

Nantucket is a way to present

The NDH E-Reader is a simple, lightweight e-reader platform, with features including dark mode, bookmarking, illustrations, audiobooks, and cover art. It is browser-based, taking up minimal bandwidth, and is mobile-responsive.

SHANTY is platform-independent. You may write in any text editor of your choosing, or in VVSTE (Very Very Simple Text Editor).

Shanty makes it a snap to convert your text files into an HTML file that fits the format of the NDH E-Reader. You don't need to spend an hour getting all the HTML tags into your story.

BASICS↑

ADDING A TITLE AND AUTHOR↑

To add a title and author to your e-book, add the following lines:

  • !!TITLE [title of e-book]
  • !!AUTHOR [atuhor of e-book]

ADDING CHAPTERS↑

Breaking down your e-book into chapters is easy. Place # before the name of each chapter.

  • #[chapter number]

If your chapter has a subtitle, use ## and put it on the next line.

  • #[chapter]
  • #[chapter subtitle]

The program will look for these symbols, and use them to build your table of contents.

Make sure every chapter title is unique!

EXTRAS↑

TEXT-ALIGN↑

Nantucket will default to justified paragraphs: each line has the same width.

If you want to align the text a different way, put the following lines at the beginning of your paragraphs:

  • :left[left-aligned text]
  • :center[center-aligned text]
  • :right[right-aligned text]

SUB-TITLES↑

To add a sub-title to your book, just add a !!SUBTITLE line to the beginning of your text:

  • !!SUBTITLE [subtitle text here]

SPECIAL CREDITS↑

The program will automatically credit the e-book as "By [author's name]."

If you want to specify a different credit, use a !!CREDIT line.

  • !!CREDIT [special credit]

DATES AND LOCATIONS↑

You may want to end your piece with some information on when and where you wrote it. This helps ground your work in a specific time and place. To do so, use the following format:

  • !!LOCATION [Add any location you want]
  • !!DATE [YYYY-MM-DD]

Nice. Just like Stephen King!

The location and date are both optional, use can use either, both, or neither.

These will always be placed towards the end of your document.

DEDICATIONS↑

To add a dedication to your e-book, add the following line:

  • @@DED [dedication here]

QUOTES AND EPIGRAPHS↑

An epigraph is a quote at the beginning of your book that sets the tone, or provides the context, for the rest of the work.

To add an epigraph, or any other special quote, use the following lines:

  • @@BLOCKQ [quote]
  • @@QUOTEATTR [quote attribution]

FOOTNOTES↑

LYRICS AND POETRY↑

If you need to add a few lines of a song or poetry to your e-book, preface each line with the ` character, like this:

  • ` [poetry line here]
  • ` [poetry line here]

Since much of a poem's meaning comes from its presentation, I understand that this may be insufficient. If a poem has to be presented a certain way, check out the later sections on customization.

ADDING ART TO YOUR E-BOOK↑

Since our media environment is almost entirely visual, it is recommended that you include images in your e-books.

For all images, remember to choose an image resolution and compression that minimizes file size, and maximizes quality. A bit of experimenting is a great help.

ADDING A COVER IMAGE↑

Can you say pizzaz? If you want to add a cover image to your e-book, add the following line:

  • @@COVERIMG [link or filepath to to cover image]

The cover image will always come first in the e-book.

If you want to include a description of the cover image, and the artist's name, include the following lines at the beginning of your document:

  • !!COVERART [name of artist]
  • !!COVERDESC [description of cover art]

ADDING ILLUSTRATIONS↑

You can add an indefinite number of illustrations to your e-book. To add an illustration, include the following line in your text:

  • @@ILLO :source [link or filepath to illustration] :orientation [either "landscape" OR "portrait"] :illustrator [name of illustrator] :desc [a brief description for the image's title and alt tags] :buy [link to site where illustration can be purchased] :caption [Text to accompany illustration when it's clicked or tapped]

Only the :source attribute is required, and if :caption is used, it must come last.

ADDING AN AUDIOBOOK↑

One of the most powerful features of the Nantucket E-Book format is the ability to have a book's text, and an audio version, together. Adding audiobooks to your e-book is much the same as adding an illustration.

PREPARING THE AUDIO SECTION↑

Before adding audio files to your e-book, start by adding an !!AUDIO tag at the beginning of your text:

  • !!AUDIO :background [link or file path to a background image] :copyright [Copyright information for audiobook]

If a background image is specified, it will be blurred and brightened to separate it from the foreground elements. If no background is specified, the program will default to a plain seashell background, like the rest of the e-book.

ADDING AUDIO FILES↑

Any number of audio files can be included. The program will accept MP3s.

Longer works, such as novels, should break the audiobook into several short audio files.

METADATA AND SEARCH ENGINE OPTIMIZATION↑

This section covers things that will make your website look more appealing to search engines, plus copyright info.

COPYRIGHT INFORMATION↑

It's a very, very good idea to include the copyright info for your e-book, even if it's 100% public domain. This furthers the legal protection for your e-book, and lets the reader know how they can share it.

To add copyright info, add the following line to the beginning of your document:

  • !!COPYRIGHT [YYYY]

If your work is public domain, use:

  • !!COPYRIGHT PUBLIC DOMAIN

This will put a strikethrough on the copyright symbol.

SYNTAX RULES

METADATA

At the beginning of your document, you should include all of the necessary metadata.

  • !!TITLE [Put title here]
  • !!SUBTITLE
  • !!CREDIT
  • !!AUTHOR
  • !!ILLUSTRATOR
  • !!COVERART
  • !!AUDIO

If you would like to include audio files with your e-book, include the !!AUDIO notice in your metadata.

Users will access the audio from the e-reader menu, by clicking on the cassette icon:

This will take users to the audio section.

!!AUDIO :background [source of background image] :copyright [recording copyright information]

  • The :background notice specifies the image you'd like for the background of the audio section. The image is stylistically altered to emphasize the foreground.
    • If no background is specified, the e-reader will default to the seashell color.
  • The :copyright notice allows you to specify the copyright status for the audio file.
  • :copyright MUST come after :background

Strictly speaking, none of this metadata is required for the NDH E-Reader format.

At the end of your document, you may want to include information on where and when your content was written.

  • !!LOCATION [Whatever you want]
  • !!DATE [YYYY-MM-DD]

Some people prefer MM-DD-YYYY formats… I for one care less for them.

CHAPTERS

"#" is used for chapter titles, and "##'' is used for chapter subtitles.

  • #Chapter One
  • ##The Morning Shift

VVSTE's text-parsing program will look for these codes, and use them to build your e-book's Table of Contents.

The NDH E-Reader uses three tables of contents: a drop-down list accessed from the menu for desktop users, a full page for mobile users, and one displayed within the page layout for accessibility.

ADDING CONTENT

"@@" is used for adding special content to your document.

COVER ART

  • @@COVERIMG [file path]

ILLUSTRATIONS

The NDH E-Reader can support an indefinite number of illustrations.

@@ILLO :small [illustration thumbnail filepath] :large [illustration filepath] :buy [link to web page where illustration can be purchased] :orientation [landscape or portrait] :caption [text]

:caption MUST come last.

AUDIO FILES

The NDH E-Reader supports an indefinite number of audio files.

@@AUDIO :source [audio filepath] :title [name for audio file]

:title MUST come last.
